Trading (Level Two)

What is a Bid Size ?

The number of shares being offered for purchase at a specified bid price, that a fantasy player (buyer) is willing to purchase at that bid price. For example, if an fantasy player (investor) wants to buy 200 contracts of Adrian Peterson at 10 per share, the bid size is 200 shares. The exchange would then quote this bid size in the hundreds, so the bid size for Adrian Peterson would be two. If the bid size was 500 shares, the bid size quote would be five.
BREAKING DOWN 'Bid Size'
Bid size is the opposite of ask size. Ask size is the amount of a security that a fantasy player is offering to sale. Bid size and ask size are thought to have a relationship which imply that if bid sizes are higher than ask sizes, then there may be a high demand for the players stock.

What is a Bid ?

A bid price is the price a fantasy player is willing to pay for a player. This is one part of the bid with the other being the bid size, which details the amount of shares the fantasy player is willing to purchase at the bid price. The opposite of the bid is the ask price, which is the price a fantasy player (seller) is looking to get for his or her shares of a player.
BREAKING DOWN 'Bid Price'
The use of bid and ask is a fundamental part of fantasybits market system, as it details the exact amount that a fantasy player could buy or sell at any point in time. Remember that the current price is not the price for which you can purchase the player (nfl player), but the price at which the player (nfl player) shares last traded hands. If you want to get an idea of the price for which you can buy a nfl players security, you need to look at the bid and ask prices because they will often differ from the current price.

What is a Ask ?

'Ask' is the price a fantasy player (seller) is willing to accept for a player, also known as the offer price. Along with the price, the ask quote will generally also stipulate the amount of the player (nfl player) willing to be sold at that price.
BREAKING DOWN 'Ask' This is the opposite of bid, which is the price a fantasy player (buyer) is willing to pay for a nfl players security, and the ask will always be higher than the bid. The terms "bid" and "ask" are used in nearly every financial market in the world covering stocks, bonds, currency and derivatives. An example of an ask in the fantasybit market would be $5.24 fantasybits x 1,000 which means that someone is offering to sell 1,000 shares for $5.24 fantasy bits.

What is Ask Size ?

The ask size is the amount of a security that a market maker is offering to sell at the ask price. The higher the ask size, the more supply there is that people want to sell. When a fantasy player (buyer) seeks to purchase a security, he or she can accept the ask price and buy up to the ask size amount at that price. If the fantasy player (buyer) wishes to acquire more of the security over the current ask size, he or she may have to pay a slightly higher price to the next available fantasy player (seller) .
BREAKING DOWN 'Ask Size'
All fantasy players are the ones who offer to buy and sell securities. The fantasy player must state the price it is asking for a given player (the ask price) and the amount it is willing to sell at that price (the ask size). Also, the fantasy player must state the price at which he or she is willing to buy the security (the bid price) and the amount of securities he or she is willing to buy (the bid size). When a customer order comes to the exchange, the order is filled by the fantasy player with the lowest ask price (for buy orders) or the highest bid price (for sell orders).
